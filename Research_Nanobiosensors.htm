Nanobiosensors

The detection of bio-active molecules with high sensitivity, specificity and small sample volumes is of interest to a variety of applications ranging from detecting hazardous bio-toxins on the battle-field to timely clinical diagnosis of disease in the patient's home. Recent success with electronic technologies based on silicon nanowires that can record specific antigen-antibody interactions has spurred immense interest in this area. In collaboration with Dr. Fan Ren (fren@che.ufl.edu),we are developing new devices based on AlGaN/GaN sensors that can detect antigen-antibody interactions with high sensitivity. These devices are advantageous owing to their ease of fabrication, low cost, and low fluid volume handling. Studies are underway to wirelessly detect a variety of bio-agents ranging from bio-toxins to cancer markers.
